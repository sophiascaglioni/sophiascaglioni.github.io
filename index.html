I am an incoming Pre-Doctoral researcher at MIT working with Nina Roussille (MIT Economics) and Anna Stansbury (MIT Sloan).

My primary research areas are labor economics and political economy. I am interested in the moral, financial, and social tradeoffs made by workers when choosing a job, as well as broader topics at the intersection of labor and policy including worker power, wage inequality, and technological change.

Before coming to MIT, I worked on the largest ever study of unconditional cash transfers in the United States at OpenResearch. I received my BA in Economics and Political Science with Honors from Boston University, where I was awarded best undergraduate thesis for my work on heterogeneous firm preferences when engaging in corporate political activity.
