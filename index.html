I am a Pre-Doctoral researcher at MIT working with Nina Roussille (MIT Economics) and Anna Stansbury (MIT Sloan).

My primary research areas are labor economics and political economy. I am interested in topics at the intersection of labor and policy including worker power, wage inequality, and technological change. I'm particularly curious about the impact of socioeconomic background on career outcomes, tradeoffs between wage and non-wage amenities of jobs, and self-reported happiness.

Before coming to MIT, I worked on the largest ever study of unconditional cash transfers in the United States at OpenResearch. I received my BA in Economics and Political Science with Honors from Boston University, where I was awarded best undergraduate thesis for my work on heterogeneous firm preferences when engaging in corporate political activity.
